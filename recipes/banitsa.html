Bantisa

Banitsa is a traditional Bulgarian pastry dish made with layers of thin filo dough, eggs, and cheese, sometimes with added ingredients like spinach, yogurt, or minced meat.
Here's a recipe for classic banitsa:

Ingredients

  • 1 package of filo pastry sheets (about 16-20 sheets)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups crumbled feta cheese (you can also use a mixture of feta and other cheeses like mozzarella or ricotta)
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: chopped fresh parsley or dill for added flavor

Instructions In Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish with butter or oil.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs and plain yogurt together until well combined. Season with salt to taste. If using, mix in chopped fresh parsley or dill for added flavor.
  3. Unroll the filo pastry sheets on a clean work surface. Cover them with a damp towel to prevent them from drying out.
  4. Take one sheet of filo pastry and place it in the prepared baking dish, allowing the edges to hang over the sides. Brush the sheet lightly with melted butter.
  5. Repeat the process with another sheet of filo pastry, laying it perpendicular to the first sheet in the baking dish. Brush it with melted butter.
  6. Continue layering filo pastry sheets and brushing each layer with melted butter until you have used about half of the sheets.
  7. Sprinkle half of the crumbled feta cheese evenly over the layered filo pastry sheets in the baking dish.
  8. Pour half of the egg and yogurt mixture over the cheese layer, spreading it evenly.
  9. Continue layering the remaining filo pastry sheets on top of the cheese and egg mixture, brushing each layer with melted butter as before.
  10. Sprinkle the remaining crumbled feta cheese evenly over the top layer of filo pastry sheets.
  11. Pour the remaining egg and yogurt mixture over the cheese layer, spreading it evenly.
  12. Fold any overhanging edges of the filo pastry over the top to enclose the filling.
  13. Brush the top of the banitsa with the remaining melted butter.
  14. Bake the banitsa in the preheated oven for about 40-45 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy and the filling is set.
  15. Once baked, remove the banitsa from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
  16. Serve the banitsa warm or at room temperature. Enjoy your delicious homemade banitsa!
