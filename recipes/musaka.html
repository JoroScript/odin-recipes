Musaka

Moussaka is a delicious dish with layers of eggplant, potatoes, minced meat, and a
creamy béchamel sauce, popular in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines.Here's a basic recipe:

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced lengthwise
  • 2 large potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 lb (450g) ground lamb or beef (you can also use a mixture of both)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions In Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Salt the eggplant slices and let them sit for about 20-30 minutes to draw out excess moisture. Then, rinse them and pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. Heat some olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the eggplant slices until they are lightly browned on both sides. Remove them from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add a bit more olive oil if needed and sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until softened.
  5. Add the ground meat to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  6. Stir in the diced tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together and the sauce thickens slightly. Remove from heat.
  7. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour to make a roux and cook for a couple of minutes until it's lightly golden.
  8. Gradually whisk in the milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Cook the sauce until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  9. Season the béchamel sauce with nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  10. Now it's time to layer the moussaka! In a greased baking dish, arrange a layer of potato slices at the bottom, followed by a layer of eggplant slices.
  11. Spread the meat mixture over the eggplant layer.
  12. Pour the béchamel sauce over the meat layer, spreading it evenly.
  13. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the top.
  14. Bake the moussaka in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly.
  15. Allow the moussaka to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy your delicious homemade moussaka!
