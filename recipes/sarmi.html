Sarmi

Sarmi, also known as Sarma, is a traditional dish in various Balkan and Middle Eastern countries, consisting of rolled cabbage or grape leaves filled
with a savory mixture typically containing rice and ground meat. Here's a recipe for cabbage rolls, one of the most common variations of sarmi:

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of cabbage
  • 1 cup rice, rinsed
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or a mixture of beef and pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions In Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Carefully add the whole head of cabbage to the boiling water and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the outer leaves are softened. Remove the cabbage from the water and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the rinsed rice, ground meat, chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.
  3. Carefully separate the softened cabbage leaves, trimming away the thick center rib if necessary to make them easier to roll.
  4. Place a spoonful of the meat and rice mixture onto each cabbage leaf, near the base. Roll the leaf tightly around the filling, tucking in the sides as you roll, to form a neat cylinder. Repeat with the remaining cabbage leaves and filling.
  5. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Arrange the cabbage rolls seam side down in the skillet, fitting them snugly together.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together the tomato paste and diced tomatoes. Pour this mixture over the cabbage rolls.
  7. Pour the beef or vegetable broth into the skillet until it almost covers the cabbage rolls.
  8. Cover the skillet and simmer the cabbage rolls over low heat for about 45-60 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the cabbage is tender.
  9. Meanwhile, prepare the sauce. In another skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and cook until softened.
  10. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  11. Once the cabbage rolls are cooked, serve them hot with the tomato sauce spooned over the top. Enjoy your delicious homemade sarmi!
